## One-paragraph summary

A single-entry conversations log dated 2026-04-17, capturing career advice Minh received from **Dat** (the Meta employee, see [[dat-huynh]]) about life at Meta: how E-level promotions work, what managers value, trust dynamics, stack ranking, and the team Minh is about to join (Facebook Integrity) ([[misc-conversations]]). The conversation happened **over lunch** per Minh's same-day journal entry ([[journal-2026-04-17]]).

## Key claims

- **E4 → E5**: execution and trust matter most. Always deliver on time. Provide a good time-estimate for any task you commit to ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **E5 → E6**: three routes — specialist (only-you-can-solve-this issues), generalist (politics-heavy), or manager (politics-heavy) ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Raising issues**: volunteer to fix; if you need diagnosis time, say "I need 1 day to diagnose, then I'll give a timeline." Managers value clear timelines because they reduce manager anxiety ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Manager incentive alignment**: "Manager is on your side, because their job is to grow people." ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Stack ranking** mostly affects Exceeds and Below buckets — other managers push Exceeds down to fit the curve; Below tends to suggest a manager-employee mismatch ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Early-career advice**: grind in the first few years; Meta is not a place to chill ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Team context**: the team Minh is joining (under Facebook Integrity) is "not too crazy" per Dat, with many senior people available to ask, and Minh will likely be paired with a senior ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Trust signal hierarchy**: micromanaging ("do you need help?" frequently) signals low trust → fewer check-ins = more trust → ultimate trust = gossip happens ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Cross-team alignment**: if someone on another team is already on a project you take on, do alignment / collaborate rather than compete ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Career capital**: in the current job market, prior project work matters more than titles. Balance short-term and long-term project picks ([[misc-conversations]]).
