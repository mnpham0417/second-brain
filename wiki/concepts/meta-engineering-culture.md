## TL;DR

A condensed model of how engineers progress and operate at Meta, sourced from a 2026-04-17 mentoring chat between Minh and [[dat-huynh]]. Three threads: **leveling rules** (E4→E5 vs. E5→E6 are different games), **manager-trust dynamics** (low check-in frequency = high trust), and **stack ranking** (mostly squeezes the Exceeds and Below tails) ([[misc-conversations]]).

## Definition

Per Dat ([[misc-conversations]]):

- **E4 → E5**: pass criterion is execution + trust. "When you say you will deliver something, you should deliver it on time." Provide a good time-estimate; this promotion is "almost guaranteed" once execution and trust are demonstrated.
- **E5 → E6**: three valid routes — *specialist* (be the only person who can solve certain issues), *generalist*, or *manager*. The non-specialist routes both involve "a lot of politics".
- **Trust signal hierarchy**: micromanaging ("do you need help?" frequently) signals low trust. As manager check-ins decrease, trust is higher. "Ultimate trust is when gossip happens."
- **Stack ranking** mostly bites at the tails: limited Exceeds slots → other managers push your Exceeds-leaning reports down; Below tends to read as a manager-employee fit problem more than a pure performance one.
- **Manager incentive alignment**: "Manager is on your side, because their job is to grow people."

## Key distinctions

- **Promotion criterion vs. dollar criterion**: this model is about how the system *says* it evaluates you, not about TC.
- **Specialist vs. generalist E5→E6**: specialist is a narrower path with less politics; the other two routes trade depth for breadth and political work ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Issue-raising etiquette**: "I'll need 1 day to diagnose, then I'll give a timeline" beats giving no timeline. Managers prefer estimates with explicit uncertainty over silence ([[misc-conversations]]).
- **Cross-team work**: "if someone from another team is already doing it, do alignment" — collaborate rather than overlap ([[misc-conversations]]).

## Examples

- The team Minh is joining (under Facebook Integrity) is described as "not too crazy", with many senior people to ask, and Minh likely paired with a senior — interpreted by Dat as a relatively favorable starting context within Meta ([[misc-conversations]]).
- "At this job market, people don't care about your titles; they care about what you have worked on in the past" — implication: pick projects that are good both short and long term ([[misc-conversations]]).
